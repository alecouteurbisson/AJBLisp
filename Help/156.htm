(integer <x>)

Function

Convert a string, character, integer or a float to an integer. Float arguments are rounded to the nearest integer. String arguments must represent a valid integer (no decimal points) and may be Octal (if a leading zero is supplied, beware) or Hexadecimal (if prefixed by 0x.)



Examples: 

> (integer 3.6)
= 4

> (integer "0xff")
= 255
